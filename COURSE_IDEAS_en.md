## Topics to Cover

### 1. Static Word Embeddings

Foundation of modern NLP - representing words as dense vectors in continuous space.

- **Word2Vec**
  - Skip-gram and CBOW architectures
  - Training techniques
  - Applications and limitations

- **GloVe (Global Vectors)**
  - Matrix factorization approach
  - Comparison with Word2Vec
  - Use cases

**Suggestions:**
- Include hands-on exercises for training embeddings on custom datasets
- Visualize embeddings using t-SNE or UMAP
- Discuss semantic relationships and analogies

---

### 2. Transformers

Modern architecture that revolutionized NLP through attention mechanisms.

- **BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)**
  - Self-attention mechanism
  - Pre-training and fine-tuning paradigm
  - Masked language modeling
  - Applications: classification, NER, QA

**Suggestions:**
- Cover the evolution: from RNNs to Transformers
- Hands-on with Hugging Face Transformers library
- Explore variants (RoBERTa, DistilBERT, etc.)
- Discuss computational costs and efficiency

### 4. CoT (Chain of Thought)

Advanced prompting technique for improving reasoning in large language models.

- Zero-shot and few-shot CoT
- Step-by-step reasoning
- Applications in complex problem-solving
- Limitations and failure modes

**Suggestions:**
- Demonstrate with practical examples (math, logic, commonsense reasoning)
- Compare with other prompting strategies
- Explore variants (Self-Consistency, Tree of Thoughts)
